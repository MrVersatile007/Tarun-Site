Research Experience

NLP Solutions for Air Traffic Speech Transcription

Federal Aviation Administration July 2025 – Present
  • Fine-tuned state-of-the-art Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) models for safety-critical Air Traffic Control (ATC) speech, reducing Word Error Rate (WER) from over 60% to 8% on out-of-distribution ATC Speech data.
  • Proposed a novel Contextual Error Rate (CER) pipeline to evaluate transcription errors based on contextual relevance to ground-truth references, demonstrating that CER outperforms WER in safety-critical domains where semantic accuracy is paramount.
  • Developed a context-aware error detection and reporting pipeline that identifies transcription errors using only pilot and controller ATC speech (without ground-truth transcripts), leveraging large language models.
  • Beyond transcription accuracy, evaluated model overconfidence and hallucination behavior, observing that fine-tuning significantly improves calibration and reduces overconfident predictions.
  • Actively investigating fundamental challenges in uncertainty quantification and reliability assessment of large language models.

Advisor: Dr. Nidhal Bouaynaya, Full Professor, Electrical & Computer Engineering

Dynamic Graph Anomaly Detection, Prediction, and Explanation

National Science Foundation January 2023 – May 2024
  • Proposed a martingale-based framework for detecting structural changes in dynamic graphs using multi-feature representations.
  • Validated performance on synthetic and real-world datasets, emphasizing feature design for robust change-point detection.
  • Developed an interpretable explanation model using additive martingales with SHAP-based feature attribution.
  • Outlined extensions for localized change detection and reduced detection delay via forecasting integration.
  • Analyzed scalability challenges and proposed future directions for efficient large-scale graph analysis.

Advisor: Dr. Shen-Shyang Ho, Full Professor, Department of Computer Science
