SPATIO-TEMPORAL TEAM SPORTS DATA SCIENCE

This paper explores the use of data-driven techniques from the spatio-temporal data science field to enhance team performance through analytics. It reviews research agendas in sports science and related computer science, identifying opportunities to expand spatio-temporal intelligence, big data, and management into team sports applications. The discussion covers team/individual data analytics, moving object databases, and game tactics, with a focus on football but with broader applicability to similar sports. While visual analytics and vision-based techniques are well-researched, they are not the central focus here. (download PDF)

INTERPRETABLE CHANGE DETECTION IN EVOLVING GRAPH

In this research paper, we tackle change-point detection in dynamic graphs, emphasizing the significance of diverse graph properties in defining the detection process. we propose a novel approach using martingale values from graph features, highlighting feature selection's critical role. we advocate employing multiple martingale tests on varied graph features to identify changing properties and explain change-points. My research validates this approach's effectiveness in maintaining a low false positive rate across different feature representations. (download PDF)
